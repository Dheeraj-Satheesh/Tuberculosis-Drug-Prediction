Drug Susceptibility Testing (DST) Results

BPaLM Regimen – Inclusion Criteria

  1. Person with age 14 years & above with new microbiologically confirmed MDR/ RRTB requiring a new course of treatment or probable MDR-TB who failed H mono/poly DR-TB treatment
  2. H/o of Drug Exposure: Person with exposure of less than one month intake of Bdq, Lzd and/ or Pa in the past
  3. Person with exposure of more than one month intake of Bdq, Lzd and/ or Pa and documented sensitivity to these drugs
  4. Person who had not failed treatment with Bdq or Lzd containing shorter or longer regimen, and sensitivity to these drugs are documented
  5. QTcF in ECG is ≤450 ms in males and ≤470 ms in females
  6. When serum electrolytes are abnormal and QTcF is >450 ms in males & >470 ms in females in baseline ECG, after correcting the electrolytes, QTcF in repeat ECG is ≤450 ms in males and ≤470 ms in females
  7. Non-lactating women, lactating women but not breast-feeding, non-pregnant women, pregnant women with <20 or <24 weeks gestation and who are willing for medical termination of pregnancy (as per latest MTP gazette notification, as applicable).

Exclusion criteria & Contra-indications

  1. Person with age below 14 years
  2. Person with documented resistance to Bdq, Lzd and/ or Pa
  3. Person with significant liver dysfunction [LFT (Liver enzymes and/ or total bilirubin); AST/ALT >3.0 × ULN and Total Bilirubin >2.0 × ULN]
  4. People with severe forms of extrapulmonary-MDR-TB like CNS TB, spinal/skeletal TB, or disseminated TB (miliary TB or TB with multiorgan involvement)
  5. Person with significant Cardiac conduction abnormalities in the heart – including structural heart disease, syncope, long QT syndrome, AV blocks, Reentry arrhythmias etc
  6. Person currently having uncontrolled cardiac arrhythmia that requires medication
  7. Person with history of additional risk factors for Torsade de Pointes, e.g. heart failure, hypokalemia, family history of long QT syndrome
  8. Baseline QTcF is >450 ms in males & >470 ms in females in baseline ECG, and if electrolytes are normal. Baseline QTcF is >450 ms in males & >470 ms in females in baseline ECG, electrolytes are abnormal and even after correcting the electrolytes QTcF in repeat ECG is not ≤450 ms in males and ≤470 ms in females

9–11 Month Shorter Oral MDR/RR-TB Regimen – Eligibility Criteria

  1. Rifampicin resistance detected
  2. MDR/RR-TB with FQ resistance not detected
  3. No prior exposure >1 month to Bdq, Lfx, Cfz, Lzd (unless susceptibility confirmed)
  4. No extensive TB disease
  5. No severe extrapulmonary MDR-TB (CNS, skeletal, disseminated)
  6. Lzd may replace Eto, including in pregnancy, with monitoring
  7. Thyroid function test not required before Lzd
  8. InhA/KatG mutations:
    • Lzd regimen valid even if both mutations present
    • Eto regimen NOT valid if both mutations present
  9. Non-lactating, non-pregnant, or <20–24 weeks gestation with MTP consent if Eto used
